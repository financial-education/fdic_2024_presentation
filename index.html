The Deposit Franchise

The bank's investment in buildings, IT, advertising, and salaries allows it to pay depositors:

$$r^d_t = \beta^{Dep} f_t$$

where $r^d_t$ is the deposit rate, $f_t$ is the short-rate (fed funds) and $\beta^{Dep} \in (0,1)$. $\beta^{Dep}$ is the Deposit Beta

$IntInc_{dt} = \alpha^{Inc}_d + \beta^{Inc}_{d,0} FedFunds_{t} +$

$+ \beta^{Inc}_{d,1} FedFunds_{t-1} + \epsilon_{dt}$


and we report:


$\beta^{Inc}_d = \beta^{Inc}_{d,0} + \beta^{Inc}_{d,1}$

$IntExp_{dt} = \alpha^{Exp}_d + \beta^{Exp}_{d,0} FedFunds_{t} +$

$+ \beta^{Exp}_{d,1} FedFunds_{t-1} + \epsilon_{dt}$


and we report:


$\beta^{Exp}_d = \beta^{Exp}_{d,0} + \beta^{Exp}_{d,1}$
